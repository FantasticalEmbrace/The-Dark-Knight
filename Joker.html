Joker

Batman crept in the cover of the night after the police were all gone. Scouring through the barbarous scene before him, he sought any clues that would lead him to the assailant. A blood-splattered banner with the words "Happy Retirement" hanging from the ceiling and broken champagne glasses all about revealed that the evening had begun with a joyous occasion. With only the beam of the flashlight he was using to examine the crime scene, he could see the soulless eyes of the unfortunate staring back at him from the pale husks that were once filled with life. Now that life was everywhere: above and below him, and the walls all around. Their eyes were now frozen with permanent fright, and he knew right away from the gruesome sight all around that they had gone through a hell that even the best writers of horror could not even conjure up. But a more mysterious clue was the playing cards scattered about the room.


It suddenly became apparent that he was not dealing with the same class of criminal that would rob banks or innocents walking in alleyways for the sake of greed. This creature found humor in chaos, pleasure in malice, and fulfillment in the fear of others. This was a monster of a different breed, much worse than those that haunted our childhood nightmares. This fact was made evident by the grins drawn on the lifeless faces distributed throughout and that the playing cards left behind were all the same: the Joker.
